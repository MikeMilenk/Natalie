  1. Amstor shopping center - Kremenchuk

    On June 27, 2022, a Russian missile hit the Amstor shopping center during a rocket attack. 21 people were killed and 59 injured. All that remains is the scrap metal from the building and the ruined human lives...

    Here you can see more information about this attack on Wikipedia

    Those who died that day will never be brought back, the only thing we can do is remember them and this crime. But the building is different, what was destroyed can be restored, moreover, it can be made even better! That is why I chose this place for my graduation project.

    A brief background: long ago, as in fairy tales, this site was used as a park. It was part of the largest park in Kremenchuk. And more recently, there was the Amstor shopping center, which is now just ashes... Since both of these functions are very important, I wanted to combine them. In my idea, a park will appear on the roof of the building, which can be accessed directly from the ground. And the courtyard, surrounded by glass curtain walling systems, will visually unite the courtyard and the space of the shopping center as a continuation of the park. This way, people will have a new area for recreation, but at the same time retain the shopping function. There will be a place to buy delicious coffee and a place to walk with it.

    Yes, it's just a concept right now, but this is how I want to see Ukraine after the war.
